Enable Unknown Sources for Downloader on Firestick & Android TV – Easy 2026 Tutorial

Many IPTV and streaming apps are not available on the Amazon Appstore or Google Play Store. To install these third-party apps on Firestick or Android TV, you must enable “Install Unknown Apps” and use the Downloader app. Follow this easy 2026 guide to set everything up in minutes.

What Is Sideloading on Firestick & Android TV?
Sideloading allows you to install APK files and third-party apps on Firestick and Android TV devices without using the Amazon Appstore or Google Play Store. It’s the most common way to install IPTV players, streaming apps, and other unavailable applications.
Unlike rooting or jailbreaking, sideloading is a standard feature available on Android-based devices and can be enabled in your device settings.
⚠️ Important: Only download APK files from trusted sources to keep your device secure.
What Is Downloader for Firestick & Android TV?
Downloader is a free app designed for Fire TV and Android TV devices that helps users install third-party APK apps easily. Created by AFTVnews, the app features a built-in browser, direct URL support, and Downloader codes for quick access to apps, IPTV players, and streaming tools unavailable in official app stores.

How to Set Up Sideloading on Firestick & Android TV

The Downloader app is one of the easiest and most popular tools for sideloading APK files on Firestick and Android TV devices. It lets you download and install third-party apps directly using a URL or Downloader code in just a few steps.

Before installing IPTV apps or other unofficial applications, you must first install the Downloader app and enable the “Install Unknown Apps” permission in your device settings.

Step 1: Install Downloader

Downloader allows Firestick users to download and install third-party apps as the device doesn’t allow downloads via the browser. The app is easy to install as it is available on the Amazon Store.

  1. Open Search: On the Firestick main screen, go to Find, then click Search.
  2. Type Downloader: Start typing Downloader, and you’ll get a suggestion on the screen. Click it to search for the app.
  3. Select the App: Downloader should now appear as the first suggestion. Click on it, and Download it.

Step 2: Prep Your Firestick

By default, Firestick blocks the installation of third-party apps for security. You will need to tweak these settings before you can install your preferred IPTV players.

Allow "Apps from Unknown Sources"

Granting this permission will let you install apps you sideload via the Downloader app.

  1. Go to Settings: From the Firestick home screen click My Fire TV.
  2. Developer Options: Select Developer options.
  3. if Developer Options are hidden, click about to enable it. if you have it skip to step :7.
  4. Click on your Fire TV Stick 7 times to unlock Developer Options.
  5. After Clicking 7 times you will see message "Developer Options Enabled".
  6. Once Developer Options are enabled, select it from the menu.
  7. Enable Installation:

    For older devices: Click Apps from Unknown Sources to change the state from Off to On.

    For Firestick Lite / Gen 3: Click the option to see a list of apps. Click Downloader to turn the state to On.

    Now, your device can install apps through Downloader.
